The Day I Almost Gave Up

When I was in junior high school, I experienced something I will never forget. It happened during my second year. At first, everything felt normal. I had a few friends, and I enjoyed my classes. But one day, everything changed.

A group of older students started making fun of me. It began with simple jokes about my appearance. I tried to ignore them, but the teasing became worse. They started calling me names in the hallway and laughing loudly whenever I passed by. I felt embarrassed, angry, and alone. I didn't tell anyone at first because I was afraid things would get worse.

The worst moment happened after school. I was walking home when the group followed me and took my backpack. They threw it into a muddy puddle and laughed while I stood there, shocked and helpless. I remember going home in tears, feeling like I didn't belong anywhere.

That night, I thought about quitting school. I had no motivation and was afraid to face them again. But then my older sister noticed something was wrong. She asked me gently, and I finally opened up to her. She immediately told our parents, and together we spoke to my school counselor the next day.

The school took it seriously. The bullies were given punishment and had to attend a counseling program. Slowly, I started feeling safe again. I joined a writing club and made new friends who supported me. That experience taught me that speaking up is not a weakness ‚Äî it's a way to protect yourself and others.

Now, whenever I see someone being bullied, I try my best to help. I don't want anyone else to feel the way I did.
