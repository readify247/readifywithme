Little Holmes and the Mystery in the Museum

Last Saturday, Little Holmes, a clever boy who loved solving puzzles, visited the city museum with his aunt. The museum was full of ancient statues, paintings, and mysterious artifacts. Everything seemed normal until a loud alarm rang through the halls. A security guard shouted, "Someone has taken the silver compass from the explorer's exhibit!"

People started to panic, but Little Holmes stayed calm. He carefully observed the room. "The thief didn't break the glass," he said. "That means it must have been someone with a key."

He talked to the guard and asked who had access to the case. "Only the staff and our guest guide today," the guard replied. Holmes quickly looked at the dusty floor and noticed something strange. There were footprints leading to the dinosaur section.

He followed the footprints and found a man in a guide uniform standing nervously behind a large T-Rex model. Holmes politely asked, "Why are you hiding here?" The man stuttered and tried to run, but the guard arrived just in time.

Inside the man's bag, they found the missing compass wrapped in a scarf. The guide admitted he wanted to sell it because it was worth a lot of money.

Thanks to Little Holmes' sharp eyes and calm thinking, the mystery was solved. The museum gave him a small detective badge as a reward. Holmes smiled and said, "It was elementary, really."
